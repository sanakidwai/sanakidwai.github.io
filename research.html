current projects

Burzio's Generalisation and Hindi-Urdu:
Some dialects of HU are often given as an exception to BG based on the availability of accusative case in passives. I show HU does comply to BG, i.e. accusative case is only found where there is an external argument present, by examining unaccusatives, dative predicates, passives, impersonals and indirect causatives. The accusative-preserving "passives" are actually impersonals with active syntax, a silent external argument and accusative case.

Structure of active impersonals in Urdu:
It is claimed that accusative case can be preserved in passives in some dialects of Hindi-Urdu. I establish that these constructions are in fact impersonals with active syntax, making the presence of accusative case unsurprising. I am currently working on the structure of these impersonals. Some interesting challenges are: the nature of the (silent?) external argument, presence of so-called passive morphology and the status of Voice.
