current projects

Active impersonals in Urdu:
It is claimed that accusative case can be preserved in passives in some dialects of Urdu. I establish that these constructions are in fact impersonals with active syntax and a pro subject, making the presence of accusative case unsurprising. I am currently working on the structure of these impersonals. Some interesting challenges are: the nature of pro, the presence of so-called passive morphology and the status of Voice.

Instrumental case in Urdu:
Instrumental case is extremely versatile, and is found on by-phrases, causees, inabilitative subjects, comitatives, instruments and materials, and locations in Urdu. I am primarily investigating the conditions on instrumental case assignment in Urdu. While inabilitative subjects and comitatives - and most likely by-phrases and causees as well - are arguments, instruments, materials and locations are adjuncts. Interestingly, this neatly follows the animacy distinction. However, all these arguments are not generated in the same position. One possibility is that inherent instrumental case is assigned within PPs, while structural instrumental case is assigned to SpecVoiceP (under restricted conditions), and that this distinction does not have anything to do with animacy.

Automatic language identification in code-switched data:
with Christopher Bryant, Li Nguyen, and Theresa Biberauer.
Nguyen and Bryant (2020) developed a tool to semi-automatically annotate transcribed bilingual code-switched Vietnamese-English data with language and parts-of-speech tags. Kidwai, Bryant, Nguyen and Biberauer (2019) used this tool for automatic language identification of social media data in another language pair, Hindi-English. This paper aims to compare the results from the two language pairs and evaluate the universal applicability of the Nguyen-Bryant tool, especially on low-resource languages. The two main points of comparison are the different linguistic structures of the languages in question (English, Vietnamese and Hindi), and the challenges posed by the two mediums (spoken data vs social media texts).
